5 Simple Ways to Prevent Water Damage

Water damage can be a homeowner's worst nightmare, leading to costly repairs, structural issues, and even mold growth. But the good news is that many instances of water damage are preventable. By taking a few proactive steps, you can save yourself a significant amount of stress and money. Here are five simple ways to protect your home from water damage.

1. Inspect Your Hoses and Pipes

The average washing machine hose has a lifespan of about five years before it's at risk of bursting. Periodically check all hoses connected to your appliances, including dishwashers, refrigerators, and washing machines. Look for cracks, bulges, or leaks. Replace any hose that shows signs of wear and tear. It's also a good practice to inspect your visible plumbing pipes for any signs of corrosion or small leaks.

2. Maintain Your Gutters and Downspouts

Clogged gutters can cause water to overflow and pool around your home's foundation, leading to basement flooding or damage to your home's siding. Regularly clear out leaves and debris from your gutters and ensure your downspouts are directing water at least six feet away from your foundation. This is a simple task that can prevent major structural damage.

3. Monitor Your Water Pressure

High water pressure can put a strain on your pipes and appliance hoses, leading to leaks and ruptures. If you notice your water pressure is too high, consider installing a water pressure regulator. This device can help protect your entire plumbing system and extend the life of your appliances.

4. Check Your Sump Pump

If your home has a sump pump, it's your first line of defense against basement flooding. Test it before the rainy season to make sure it's working properly. Simply pour a bucket of water into the sump pit to ensure the pump turns on, removes the water, and then shuts off. Also, consider installing a battery backup for your pump in case of a power outage during a storm.

5. Seal Cracks in Your Foundation

Over time, small cracks can form in your home's foundation. While many are harmless, some can allow water to seep into your basement or crawl space. Regularly inspect your foundation, both inside and out, and use hydraulic cement or a sealant to fill any cracks you find. This small repair can prevent a big problem down the line.

Navigating Your Water Damage Insurance Claim

The moments following water damage can be chaotic and stressful. After you've secured your property and called for professional help, the next challenge is navigating the insurance claim. Dealing with adjusters, paperwork, and policy fine print can be overwhelming. This is where a restoration company like A&E Restoration can be your most valuable partner.

Understanding Your Policy

Your first step is to understand what your policy covers. Most standard homeowner's insurance policies cover sudden and accidental water damage, such as a burst pipe or a malfunctioning water heater. However, they typically do not cover damage from floods, a lack of maintenance, or groundwater seepage. Knowing your policy's specifics is key to a successful claim.

The Claim Process Made Simple
